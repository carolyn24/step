About Me

My name is Carolyn Akua Asante-Dartey (born May 24, 2000) and I use she/her pronouns. I come from Accra, Ghana. I am an international student at Stanford University, where I am a rising sophomore, studying Computer Science. Some things that I like to do are reading, swimming, cooking, and watching movies and TV shows. I love to listen to music, and I love to challenge myself by learning new skills. I attended Alsyd Academy for primary school, Wesley Girls High School for my first two years of high school, and Lincoln Community School, where I got my IB diploma. In school, my favourite classes are usually STEM classes. To find out more about my professional life, click here!
I come from a family of 5, I have a younger brother and a younger sister. I speak two languages; English, and my local language Twi. Back home, I was a member of the church band and I played the saxophone at my church every weekend. Some genres of music I enjoy are AfroBeats, RnB, pop, hip hop and jazz music. I love to draw and design as well. Some communities at Stanford that I am a part of include the Stanford African Students Association, where I work as the Financial Officer, the Society of Black Scientists and Engineers and Women in Computer Science. I also love to travel with my friends and family. Click here to find out more about my travel adventures!
Some of my favorite TV Shows are Prison Break, Suits, How to Get Away With Murder, All American, and Merlin. I am a huge fan of the Avengers and I love to go for concerts. I like to meeet new people, make new friends and learn about their backgrounds. I believe every person is unique and has a special element.
